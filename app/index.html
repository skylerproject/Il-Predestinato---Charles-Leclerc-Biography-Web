The Promise

"Just a week before my father died, I lied to him saying I had signed with Ferrari. Not for 2018, but for 2019. Obviously I hadn't done it, but I promised that I would have given everything to achieve this goal. My mother said to me, 'This is not good Charles, you shouldn't lie to him.' But I knew he would have left us within the next week. He himself knew he was at the end. To give him one last satisfaction, I told him I had signed. I remember the tears in his eyes. From that day on I took a remorse with me. Then, when I actually signed up, it was the first thing I thought of: 'I didn't lie that day.'"

— Charles Leclerc
Biography

The Road to Greatness

From the karting tracks of the Côte d'Azur to the hallowed pitlane at Maranello — every chapter was written in speed, loss, and defiance.

1997–2013

The Early Spark

Born in Monte Carlo on October 16, 1997, Charles grew up in the shadow of the Grand Prix barriers. Mentored by Jules Bianchi — his godfather, hero, and guardian angel — young Leclerc dominated European karting alongside future rival Max Verstappen. The loss of Jules in 2015, and his father Hervé in 2017, forged an unbreakable will to race not just for himself — but for them.

2016–2017

Dominating the Ladder

A masterclass ascent. GP3 Champion in 2016, then a devastating F2 Championship campaign with Prema in 2017 — seven victories, zero doubt. Days after his father passed away, Charles won in Baku. The Ferrari Driver Academy's crown jewel graduated with honours.

2018–2022

The Dream & The Struggle

A debut at Sauber turned every head. Ferrari called — and the legendary 2019 Monza victory saw 100,000 tifosi erupt. But the years that followed tested him: unreliable machinery, strategic catastrophes, championship near-misses. Through it all he never stopped pushing. The struggle deepened the legend.

2025–2026

The Superteam Era

History arrives at Maranello. Lewis Hamilton — seven-time World Champion — joins Leclerc to form the most formidable pairing in a generation. The SF26 is Ferrari's boldest machine in years. Two lions share one cage. The drought must end.

Breaking the Curse

The Ghost of Monaco (2017–2023)

For years, Monte Carlo was a theatre of heartbreak for its favourite son. Mechanical failures, strategic disasters, and a crash from pole in 2021. Year after year: will he ever win here?

Redemption (2024)

May 26, 2024. Rain and thunder. Charles Leclerc crossed the line first at the Monaco Grand Prix — the curse shattered. Tears behind the visor. Then came Monza again — confirming 2024 as his year of reckoning.

"We did it. For Dad. For Jules."

More Than
a Racing Driver

Away from the circuits, Charles is a man of quiet depth. A pianist who finds solace in Chopin after a bruising qualifying. A face of haute couture, gracing Giorgio Armani campaigns with the same poise he carries through Monaco's chicanes.

In February 28, 2026, Charles reached his most personal milestone — his wedding with Alexandra Saint Mleux, a celebration of love that mirrored the triumph on track. On the streets where he races, he now walks with the partner who has been his anchor through every triumph and heartbreak. The couple kept the event private, featuring close friends and family, in Monaco of course. And were spotted leaving in a vintage 1957 Ferrari 250 Testa Rossa.

Life in Milestones

The Complete Journey

From a hospital in Monte Carlo to the most iconic cockpit in motorsport — every moment shaped Il Predestinato.

1997

Born in Monte Carlo

October 16, 1997 — Charles Marc Hervé Perceval Leclerc is born in the Principality of Monaco. His father, Hervé Leclerc, is a former Formula 3 driver. Racing was not a choice — it was destiny.

2005

The Karting Prodigy

Begins competitive karting at age 8. Mentored by his godfather, Jules Bianchi, Charles tears through the European karting scene, battling future F1 rival Max Verstappen race after race. Multiple French and European titles follow.

2015

Losing Jules

Jules Bianchi — mentor, godfather, hero — passes away from injuries sustained at the 2014 Japanese Grand Prix. Charles carries his memory forward, vowing to reach F1 in his honour.

2016

GP3 Series Champion

In his debut season in single-seaters, Leclerc dominates the GP3 Series with ART Grand Prix — four wins, seven podiums, and the championship title. The Ferrari Driver Academy takes notice.

2017

The White Lie & F2 Glory

His father Hervé is diagnosed with a terminal illness. Determined to shield Charles from distraction, Charles tells his father a "white lie" — that he'll take it easy, not push too hard. Instead, he fights like a man possessed. Days after Hervé passes away, Charles wins in Baku, dedicating the victory to his father. He goes on to win the FIA Formula 2 Championship with Prema Racing.

2018

Formula 1 Debut — Sauber

Graduates to Formula 1 with Alfa Romeo Sauber. Immediately outperforms his experienced teammate Marcus Ericsson, scoring points in his debut season and earning a call-up to the senior Ferrari team.

2019

Scuderia Ferrari — The Dream Realised

The youngest Ferrari driver in 50 years. Wins at Spa-Francorchamps (dedicating it to the late Anthoine Hubert) and Monza — where 100,000 tifosi erupt. Two victories, seven poles. A star is born in red.

2024

Monaco GP Victory — The Curse Broken

After years of heartbreak in his home race, Leclerc finally wins the Monaco Grand Prix. Then doubles down with victory at Monza. "For Dad. For Jules." The emotion is overwhelming.

2025

The Superteam Forms

Lewis Hamilton arrives at Maranello. Two of the greatest drivers of their generation united under the Prancing Horse. The SF26 is unveiled — Ferrari's most ambitious machine yet. The championship drought must end.
